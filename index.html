About Me

I grew up in Colorado Springs, Colorado where I learned to love the outdoors and running. I started running in high school and walked on to the cross-country team at the University of Northern Colorado. After a few stress fractures, I gave up cross-country to join the Athletic Training program. Athletic Training has provided many opportunities to travel and explore the world around me. From Indiana to China to Guatemala to Ohio, the adventures continue. After moving to Ohio, I met my fiance, Parker. With the wedding coming, the adventures continue. My free time includes running, knitting, cooking, and learning to program. I love running marathons and half-marathons. Occasionally, I will run other distances. I have a secret love of Jelly Beans and almost anything sweet. On the weekend, you can find Parker and I in our favorite local coffee shop.

Getting Back into Running

January 22, 2017

I've been working on getting back into running after the unfortuante ice fall a little over a week ago. Unfortuantely, it has been a little more challenging than I expected due to a knee that is also now hurting. I've resigned to run about once every other day. However, today the weather was lovely, so I made sure to try and get a long run in. It was amazing. I ran 8 miles, and felt good for most of it. Unfortuately, tonight, my knee is a little sore and I can feel my bruised tailbone. However, it was good to be back into it. This week I am super busy with wedding related items, so it might be a little struggle to find time to run each day. But I'll do my best to try and get at least thirty minutes of exercise each day.

Nuun Hydration Team

January 19, 2017

Exciting news! I just received my email from Nuun that I have been accepted to be a part of their ambassador team. I am super excited to be running for Nuun. My adventure with using Nuun began as I was training for Boston. I was struggling with significant fatigue during my training runs and due to my issues with heartburn, I couldn't each much before I went out for training runs. I spoke with a dietitian who recommended trying to use electrolytes and water during my runs to combat the fatigue. I tried quite a few different brands, however, due to the fizziness of most electrolyte solutions, I struggled to drink most brands without having significant heartburn. However, I tried Nuun and found if I let it sit overnight, I could drink it without issue. It has made a significant improvement in my fatigue levels. I cannot wait to share more about how I have seen Nuun help my running!

Black Ice: WHOOPS!

January 12, 2017

Winter has arrived here in Ohio, and with it, my favorite season of running. I love bundling up to head outside for a long run. I also love the burning feeling you get in your lungs when you finish a solid hard effort in the cold. And, you can't beat running on fresh snow. However, the one aspect I do not like is the ice. Ice with a layer of fresh snow tends to be ok to run on, but sheer ice is not good. Tuesday morning, we woke up to freezing rain. As I'm not a fan of the treadmill, I told myself I would at least try and run. However, as soon as I was out of the apartment parking lot, I realized the sidewalks were super icy. I turned around and did a HIIT workout from the comfort of our living room. Yesterday, the rain had stopped and the weather was projected to warm up. As I started running, I noticed some spots were icy, however, it was not as bad as Tuesday morning. I made it 6 miles without difficulty. However, 2 streets from home, I had to stop while on ice for a turning car â€‹. Naturally shifting my momentum backwards caused me to fall backwards. Aside from an incredibly sore butt, I walked away with little issue. Unfortunately, 1 day post injury, I can barely walk and running is out of the question. I'll be taking a hiatus from running to heal up my little behind. It was not the perfect texture for running yesterday.
